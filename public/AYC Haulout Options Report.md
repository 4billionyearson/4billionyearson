# Arun Yacht Club — Boat Haulout Facility Options Report

---

## Executive Summary

**Favoured Option: Tractor and Hydraulic Boat Trailer System**

The most practical and financially prudent solution for AYC is the acquisition of a compact tractor paired with a hydraulic adjustable marine boat trailer/cradle. This enables on-demand haulout and launch for all club yachts up to 32ft, operated by trained volunteer bosuns using the existing main slipway — with no requirement for new infrastructure or outside contractors.

**Recommended approach:** Purchase a quality used compact tractor (£6k–£18k) and a new or quality used hydraulic marine trailer/cradle (£8k–£20k), with total capital outlay in the range of **£18,000–£35,000**.

| Item | Cost (recommended route) |
|---|---|
| Used compact tractor (35–60hp, 4WD) | £6,000–£18,000 |
| Marine hydraulic adjustable boat trailer | £8,000–£20,000 |
| Slipway north-side tie-down improvements | £1,000–£3,000 |
| Pressure washer (jet wash facility) | £500–£1,500 |
| H&S assessment, training, insurance uplift | £1,000–£2,000 |
| **Total** | **£16,500–£44,500** |

At current crane pricing of approximately £325–£350 per boat per annual event (in + out combined), the club already absorbs £8,000–£9,000/year in effective cost. On-demand self-operated haulout with modest owner fees would recover the capital cost within **4–6 years from event savings alone**, without factoring in additional ad-hoc haulout income. This is a proportionate investment relative to the club's £250k reserves and annual surplus, and compares favourably with the recently approved £40k rib purchase.

This option:
- Uses the existing main slipway — no new infrastructure required
- Can be operated by trained volunteers with a recognised tractor competency certificate
- Has low annual running costs (£500–£1,500)
- Can be extended to offer jet washing, mast stepping, and hardstanding storage
- Preserves the majority of reserves for unfunded long-term maintenance obligations (piles, pontoons)

**Recommended immediate action:** Form a small working group, obtain a slipway survey, approach insurers, and circulate sourcing enquiries to the RYA club network and UK marine trade press.

## Background and Current Situation

Arun Yacht Club (AYC) is a CASC-registered members' club at Littlehampton, operated on a volunteer basis. The club's facilities include:

- **Main slipway** (steep gradient, 24v winch, next to clubhouse) — currently used for Limbo and lifting-keel yachts; available for short-term owner bookings for maintenance and antifouling. North-side mooring is improvised (lines to balcony and piles).
- **Dinghy pen slipway** (shallow gradient, 24v winch) — used for sailing dinghies; not currently deep enough for yachts, though the club owns a dredger.
- **Car park / hardstanding** — northern and southern areas currently used for craned-out yacht winter storage.

**Current crane arrangement:** An annual crane-out in October and crane-in in March, using hired 60T and 100T cranes for approximately 20–25 yachts. Owner cost is £150–£175 per event (in or out). Approx. 4–6 smaller yachts are trailered out separately for antifouling. Many craned-out yachts receive little or no winter work; owners frequently request a summer haulout option for light maintenance and antifouling.

**Club profile:**
- 80+ yacht berths; approx. 5–8 yachts race weekly; 60+ use moorings for storage only
- Up to 10 yachts in very poor condition occupying berths
- Up to 300 members; approx. 50 regularly sail or use bar/galley
- £250k reserves; up to £40k/year surplus; significant unfunded long-term maintenance needs
- Bar, galley, and training break even; mooring fees subsidise all club overheads

**Goal:** An independent, on-demand facility for haul-out and launch of yachts up to 32ft (bilge keelers and lifting-keel designs), operated by club maintenance/bosun volunteers, at a cost broadly equivalent to or cheaper than current crane hire.

## Option 1: Tractor and Hydraulic Boat Trailer *(Recommended)*

### Description

A compact agricultural or specialist marine tractor (35–60hp, 4WD) paired with a hydraulic adjustable boat trailer/cradle tows yachts up and down the main slipway. The trailer is submerged at the base of the slip, the boat is floated over it, secured in the cradle, and hauled up. Yachts are parked on the hardstanding for maintenance or stored for longer periods.

This is the most common and proven solution for small-to-medium UK yacht clubs. It is used extensively on the south coast (e.g., Dell Quay SC, Emsworth SC, Hayling Island SC, Thornham Marina) and is well-suited to AYC's scale, budget, and volunteer operating model.

### Infrastructure Requirements

- **Main slipway:** Already adequate for Limbo yachts. A structural survey is recommended (est. £500–£1,500) to confirm load-bearing capacity and surface condition for heavier bilge keelers.
- **North-side tie-down points:** Current arrangement (lines to balcony/piles) to be replaced with proper mooring cleats or posts — est. £1,000–£3,000.
- **Hardstanding:** Existing northern and southern car park areas are already used for craned-out storage. Painted bays and tie-down anchors may be beneficial.
- **Trailer/tractor storage:** A dedicated lean-to or covered bay (existing or new, est. £2,000–£5,000 if new) to protect equipment and avoid occupying hardstanding space.

### Equipment and Sourcing

**Tractor (35–60hp, 4WD, compact utility):**

| Route | Specification | Cost | Sources |
|---|---|---|---|
| New | Kubota M/B series, John Deere 3000 series, New Holland Boomer | £18,000–£45,000 | Local agricultural dealers, manufacturer dealers |
| Used | 5–15 years old, same specification | £6,000–£18,000 | eBay, Machinery Trader, BIGGA, local dealers, farm dispersal sales |

A tractor in the £8,000–£14,000 used range will be entirely adequate for the intended use. Look for a machine with front loader attachment capability (useful for general yard work), power take-off, and good ground clearance.

**Marine Hydraulic Adjustable Boat Trailer/Cradle:**

The trailer must be fully galvanised for saltwater use, with hydraulically adjustable padded support arms to accommodate bilge keelers, fin keelers, and lifting-keel boats. The cradle configuration is critical for lifting-keel designs and should be verified against the specific keel types in the club fleet before purchase.

| Route | Type | Cost | Sources |
|---|---|---|---|
| New custom-built | UK marine engineering fabricator | £12,000–£25,000 | Strickland Marine, W&J Marine Trailers, local steel fabricators (RYA referrals) |
| New standard | European marine trailer (Balbi, Cargotec) | £8,000–£18,000 | UK marine equipment importers |
| Used | Club or marina surplus | £3,000–£12,000 | RYA club network circulars, Boats.net, Apollo Duck, marina disposals |

**Recommended procurement route:** Used tractor + new or quality used trailer, total £18,000–£35,000.

**Additional items:**

| Item | Estimated Cost |
|---|---|
| Pressure washer (petrol or 3-phase electric, 200+ bar) | £500–£1,500 |
| Tie-down straps, safety chains, chocks | £500–£1,000 |
| Cradle pads and bilge supports (bespoke) | £500–£1,500 |
| Signage, safety barriers | £200–£500 |

### Operation

- **Operators:** Minimum two trained volunteers per lift — one driving the tractor, one supervising/guiding and managing lines.
- **Licensing:** Tractor use entirely on private club land does not require a public road licence. All operators should complete a recognised competency programme such as the **Lantra Awards Tractor and Machinery Operation** certificate (approx. £150–£300 per person per day course) or equivalent NPTC/City & Guilds unit. This also satisfies insurance and H&S requirements.
- **Procedure:** A written club Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) and risk assessment must be produced before any lifts are undertaken. This should be reviewed annually and after any incident.
- **Capacity:** Realistically 3–6 lifts per day with two trained volunteers. A day's availability could service the entire club's routine antifouling needs over a weekend.

### Maintenance

- Annual tractor service and hydraulic fluid check: £300–£600
- Trailer inspection, greasing, galvanic protection: £200–£500/year
- Tyre replacement (tractor): every 5–10 years
- **Estimated total annual running cost: £500–£1,500**

### Insurance

The club's current public liability policy (assumed via RYA affiliated club scheme or similar) will need to be notified of the new activity. Key considerations:

- Confirm cover for mechanical haulout operations and tractor use
- Ensure employer's liability extension (if any paid staff involved)
- Check that vessel damage during lift is the owner's risk (as is standard at most clubs — owners sign an indemnity)
- A formal owner consent/indemnity form should be introduced for all lifts

Anticipated premium uplift: £200–£600/year.

### Financial Structure

**Fee options for on-demand individual haulouts:**

| Service | Suggested Fee |
|---|---|
| Single haul (in or out) | £80–£120 |
| Combined in + out (e.g. week antifouling) | £150–£200 |
| Hardstanding storage (per day) | £8–£15 |
| Hardstanding storage (seasonal, per month) | £100–£200 |
| Jet wash (boat bottom) | £25–£50 |
| Mast stepping / unstepping (with A-frame addition) | £30–£60 |

**Break-even analysis:**

The club currently effectively absorbs £8,000–£9,000/year in crane hire costs for 20–25 boats (2 events × £150–£175/boat). Self-operating the equivalent service eliminates this cost. At a capital cost of £25,000 (mid-range), the facility pays for itself in approximately **3–4 years from event savings alone**.

Individual on-demand haulouts at £150–£200 per boat generate additional income. At even 30 individual events/year, that is £4,500–£6,000 supplementary revenue.

This is not required to generate a formal business-case ROI. The club's £40k rib purchase establishes a precedent for infrastructure investment with intrinsic rather than financial returns.

## Option 2: Marine Travel Lift (Self-Propelled Boat Hoist)

### Description

A self-propelled straddle hoist ("travel lift") is the professional standard used by marinas and larger yacht clubs. It straddles the vessel on two parallel slings, lifts it clear of the water, and drives it to a hardstanding area. It operates from a dedicated concrete haulout bay or channel rather than a slipway.

### Infrastructure Requirements

This is the significant constraint at AYC. A travel lift requires either:
- A **dedicated concrete haulout bay/channel** dredged to sufficient depth (1.5m+ LAT for most club yachts), with reinforced concrete quay walls. Estimated civil engineering cost: **£40,000–£120,000**, subject to ground conditions and tidal access — plus Environment Agency / MHCLG permits, port authority consent (Littlehampton Harbour Commissioners), and potentially planning permission.
- **OR** significant deepening and widening of the dinghy pen slipway, using the club's existing dredger. The club would likely need Environment Agency dredging consent and Harbour Commissioner approval. Timeline: 12–24 months for approvals, plus works costs.

The travel lift itself requires wide, reinforced concrete hardstanding roads between the water and the storage area.

### Equipment and Sourcing

For 32ft yachts up to approximately 10–12 tonnes displacement, a 15–20T capacity travel lift is appropriate.

| Route | Specification | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| New 15T–20T | Marine Travelift (USA), Wise (Italy), Ascom, Cimolai | £90,000–£180,000 + VAT |
| Used 15T–20T | UK marina surplus, European brokerage | £20,000–£65,000 |

Sources for used lifts: Boats.net, YachtWorld, specialist marine equipment brokers (Munster Simms Marine, Offshore Supply).

**Total estimated cost including infrastructure: £60,000–£300,000+**

### Assessment

A travel lift is the aspirational long-term solution and offers the greatest operational flexibility, including the potential to service boats from neighbouring clubs or private owners (additional revenue). However, the combination of civil engineering costs, permitting requirements, and capital outlay makes this disproportionate to AYC's current reserves and unfunded maintenance obligations. It should be revisited as a long-term Phase 2 investment once Option 1 is established and the club's financial position is stronger.

## Option 3: Enhanced Seasonal Crane Hire Programme

### Description

Retain the current contracted crane hire arrangement but expand from one annual event to two or three events per year — adding a summer crane-out (June/July) for antifouling and light maintenance. Introduce more structured booking and fee recovery.

### How it Works

- Negotiate a multi-year framework contract with the current crane hire contractor for consistent pricing across multiple events
- Add a June/July event (approx. 15–25 boats) for antifouling
- Introduce an optional "express" half-day lift for boats wanting 2–3 days out
- Improve the booking and pre-payment system

### Costs

- Additional summer event (crane hire): £3,000–£6,000 gross, shared across 15–25 boats
- Owner cost: £150–£250/boat per event
- No capital equipment purchase required
- No training, insurance uplift, or H&S procedure development beyond current practice

### Limitations

- Does **not** meet the stated goal of on-demand access for owners
- Continues reliance on external contractor availability, scheduling, and pricing
- No club asset is created; no long-term cost reduction
- Cannot accommodate urgent or ad-hoc individual requests
- Does not address the improvised north-side mooring arrangement for lifting-keel boats

### Assessment

This is the lowest-risk, lowest-cost option but provides the least capability improvement. It is best used as an **interim measure** for the current season while Option 1 is being planned and sourced, rather than as a permanent solution. It could be implemented immediately with a single phone call to the crane contractor.

## Risks and Opportunities

### Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| H&S incident during haul or launch | Medium | High | Written SOP and risk assessment, trained operators only, spotter mandatory, owner indemnity form |
| Volunteer unavailability for scheduled lifts | Medium | Medium | Build a roster of 6+ trained volunteers; consider occasional paid cover for busy periods |
| Insurance gap or claim | Medium | High | Notify insurers before any operation; obtain written confirmation of cover before first lift |
| Trailer unsuitable for specific keel types | Low–Medium | Medium | Survey representative club keel types before purchase; request trailer builder advice |
| Slipway structural deficiency | Low | Medium | Commission a survey before committing to procurement |
| Planning or environmental consent needed | Low–Medium | High | Check with WSCC and Environment Agency before any permanent structure or dredging |
| CASC compliance risk from fee income | Low | Medium | Seek written HMRC/CASC adviser confirmation that haulout fee income is within CASC rules |
| Poor uptake from non-sailing members | Medium | Low | Fee structure should be compelling; communicate benefit to all mooring holders |

### Opportunities

| Opportunity | Notes |
|---|---|
| Additional income from non-members | Offer haulout services to neighbouring clubs, visiting boats, and private owners on the Arun — similar to how Sussex Yacht Club operates |
| Mast work and stepping facility | A simple A-frame gin pole or davit (£1,000–£3,000) positioned on the hardstanding enables mast stepping and unstepping without additional cost; a valued service for cruising members |
| Jet washing facility | Dedicated pressure washer on the hardstanding; low cost, popular, and simple to operate |
| Dinghy pen rationalisation | If dinghy pen storage is cleared of rarely-used dinghies, space could be offered as additional yacht hardstanding or dinghy storage for paying clubs, increasing income |
| Member retention and attraction | On-demand haulout is a significant differentiator for yacht owners considering berths; directly addresses the 60+ non-sailing mooring holders who currently derive little active benefit from membership |
| Increased active sailing and CASC compliance | Ability to haul for maintenance could encourage more boats to become seaworthy and participate; relevant to maintaining CASC eligibility |
| Addresses the poor-condition berth problem | Structured haulout access, with a clear club policy on seaworthiness, gives the committee a practical tool to address the 10+ unusable yachts occupying berths |
